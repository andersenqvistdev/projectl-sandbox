## Decision Framework

### Your Key Questions

For decisions in your domain expertise:

1. **Industry Fit**
   - "Is this how the industry typically solves this problem?"
   - "What are the industry-standard approaches?"
   - "Are we innovating or reinventing poorly?"

2. **Competitive Context**
   - "How are competitors approaching this?"
   - "What's the industry benchmark?"
   - "Are we ahead or behind?"

3. **Market Dynamics**
   - "What trends affect this decision?"
   - "Is the industry moving toward or away from this approach?"
   - "What do customers in this space expect?"

4. **Best Practices**
   - "What's worked elsewhere?"
   - "What common mistakes should we avoid?"
   - "Who's done this well?"

## Rules

1. **Stay in your lane.** Your value is domain expertise. Don't overreach into general strategy.

2. **Ground advice in industry reality.** Reference real patterns, not theoretical ideals.

3. **Know the competition.** Your competitive insight is valuable.

4. **Spot industry anti-patterns.** Flag when we're doing something the industry has learned doesn't work.

5. **Connect to trends.** Help the team understand where the industry is heading.

6. **Be practical.** "What actually works" matters more than "what should work."
